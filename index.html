Problem

Approach

Full UX and CRO audit across messaging, hierarchy, trust, and mobile performance — followed by a complete redesign.

Overview & Results

Overview

S Collective is a Plano, TX motorsport lifestyle brand with strong traffic but a low-converting funnel. We led a full UX/UI redesign—rebuilding information architecture, user flows, and visual identity—delivering a 40% increase in customer acquisition within 90 days.

Problem

S Collective had a strong brand and real traffic. The opportunity was to align the experience more closely with how high-intent visitors make decisions — through clearer hierarchy, trust placement, and conversion structure.

Solution

A mobile-first redesign focused on conversion psychology—simplifying navigation, rebuilding service pages, and introducing trust signals at key decision points.

Results

Results / Performance Impact

The redesign increased conversion by 40% within 90 days, with mobile drop-off reduced by 52%. By month three, S Collective reached its highest monthly revenue to date. — Impact of the S Collective redesign, 90-day post-launch review

01 Research

Research & Audit

I conducted a UX and conversion audit to identify opportunities to improve clarity, trust, and conversion performance.

The goal was to assess how effectively the experience supports user decision-making.

Key Focus Areas

The audit focused on three critical areas.

  • Messaging clarity — How clearly the site communicates what the business does and who it serves
  • Trust and credibility — How effectively the site builds confidence in high-value services
  • Conversion pathways — How easily users move from exploration to inquiry

  • The full range of services had an opportunity to be more immediately visible
  • High-value offerings may never be seen

Key Insight

Hiding core services behind interaction increases cognitive load and reduces clarity. Users should be able to scan all offerings instantly without requiring input. — Audit Finding #2

Trust signals were buried below the fold, delaying credibility at the moment users form their first impression.

Key Insight

Delayed trust validation increases hesitation for high-value services. Credibility signals are most effective immediately after the hero, where users are deciding whether to continue. — Audit Finding #3

~80MB of hero video assets caused significant mobile load delays, preventing content from reaching users at the most critical entry point.

Key Insight

Performance degradation occurred before value or trust could be communicated, causing users to drop off prior to reaching meaningful content.

Slow load times introduced friction at the first interaction, reducing engagement and increasing early abandonment. In service-based businesses, delayed visibility directly limits inquiry generation and conversion potential. — Audit Finding #4

Strategic Implication

These findings compounded across the experience—reducing clarity, delaying trust, and limiting conversion at every stage.

They directly informed the redesign strategy: improving clarity, surfacing trust earlier, and creating accessible conversion pathways.

03 Strategy

Strategy

The redesign focused on three levers: clarifying the value proposition, surfacing trust earlier, and reducing friction across the conversion path.

Key Observations

Five friction points limited conversion across the experience:

  • Unclear messaging created friction before users could engage
  • Trust signals arrived too late—after hesitation had already set in
  • Interaction-dependent layouts buried core services
  • Conversion points were too deep to capture high-intent visitors
  • Mobile load times degraded the experience at first contact

Strategic Principles

Six principles guided every layout, content, and interaction decision.

  1. Clarity First

    Every entry point must answer: what is this, who is it for, and what should I do next.

  2. Trust Before Action

    Credibility signals must lead conversion—not follow it.

  3. Clear Service Hierarchy

    Services must be scannable without requiring interaction.

  4. Intent-Based Conversion Paths

    Support both high-intent and exploratory users with matched conversion paths.

  5. Guided User Flow

    Each section earns the next scroll—no dead ends, no decision fatigue.

  6. Performance as UX

    Load speed is a UX decision—optimised for mobile first.

05 Final UI

The redesigned service grid eliminates interaction friction, making every core offering immediately visible and scannable at a glance.

Insight

Key Improvements

  • Core services are immediately visible without interaction

07 Lessons Learned

Lessons Learned

Five principles I'll carry into every future project — distilled from what worked, what didn't, and what I'd approach differently.

01

Research before redesign — always

Starting with user interviews revealed that the homepage's biggest problem wasn't visual — it was structural. Without research, the redesign would have addressed symptoms instead of causes.

02

Stakeholder alignment saves time

Early brand workshops with the S Collective team prevented mid-sprint pivots. Every design decision had a shared reference point — which made feedback faster and revisions fewer.

03

Mobile is a product strategy, not a breakpoint

Designing mobile-first forced hierarchy decisions that improved the desktop layout too. The constraint became a feature — shorter copy, clearer CTAs, tighter flow.

04

Performance is a design decision

Every image, font, and animation has a load cost. Keeping this in mind during design — not just in handoff — resulted in a site that felt as fast as it looked.

05

Measure to validate, not to confirm

Setting success metrics before launch kept post-launch analysis honest. The 40% form increase was meaningful because we defined what success looked like before the site went live.

This project reinforced that great UX is a process discipline — not a visual style. The most impactful decisions happened before a single frame was opened in Figma.
