Overview

Translating Real-World Demand Into a High-Performance Digital Experience

S-Collective is a Plano, TX motorsport lifestyle brand operating at the intersection of high-performance driving and premium automotive services. With strong traffic and established brand credibility, the opportunity was to better align the digital experience with how high-intent users explore automotive services and make decisions.

I was brought on as a Product Design Consultant to support a focused redesign across the Homepage, Racing Page, and new supporting pages—refining the information architecture, clarifying conversion pathways, and improving the overall on-page experience.

The objective was to create a more structured, conversion-focused foundation designed to increase engagement, improve lead quality, and support measurable growth post-launch.

Performance tracking was planned through Google Analytics 4 and Microsoft Clarity to validate impact after launch.

The Problem

Clear Demand, Opportunity for a More Structured Conversion Experience

S-Collective had strong demand and a well-established brand foundation, with an opportunity to better align the digital experience with how users evaluate high-trust, high-ticket services.

Areas identified for improvement included:

  • Clarifying service segmentation across track, automotive, and vehicle sales offerings
  • Strengthening visual hierarchy to better prioritize information and primary conversion points
  • Introducing more consistent trust signals at key decision moments
  • Structuring conversion pathways to better support different user intents
  • Adding new supporting pages to increase conversion and user retention

The focus was not to add more, but to refine the experience—reducing friction and guiding users with greater clarity and confidence.

Research & Audit

Early Insights That Shaped the Conversion Strategy

The process began with a structured review of S-Collective's existing website, focused on understanding how the current experience supports first impressions, builds trust, and guides users toward inquiry.

This early discovery helped surface key areas where the experience could be further refined before moving into design exploration.

Key Focus Areas

The review centered around three core areas:

Messaging Clarity — How clearly the site communicates its services, positioning, and target audience

Trust & Credibility — How effectively the experience builds confidence in high-value, high-trust services

Conversion Pathways — How intuitively users move from initial exploration to taking action

Research Approach

Findings were organized to identify patterns across messaging, visual hierarchy, and conversion flow—helping inform a more structured and cohesive experience moving forward.

Visually strong, but no value proposition and no CTA above the fold. First-time visitors had no clear next step.

Key Observations:

  • An opportunity to more explicitly communicate S Collective's services and specialties
  • No primary or secondary call-to-action is visible on first load
  • Users can engage with visuals without understanding how to proceed

Key Insight

Without a clear value proposition or visible next step, users are forced to self-navigate. This increases friction early in the experience and reduces conversion potential.

— Audit Finding #1

Services were hidden behind a carousel. Users had to interact before understanding what was on offer — most never did.

Key Observations:

  • Users must interact to view the full range of services
  • The full range of services had an opportunity to be more immediately visible
  • High-value offerings may never be seen

Key Insight

Hiding core services behind interaction increases cognitive load and reduces clarity. Users should be able to scan all offerings instantly without requiring input.

— Audit Finding #2

Trust signals were buried below the fold, delaying credibility at the moment users form their first impression.

Key Insight

Delayed trust validation increases hesitation for high-value services. Credibility signals are most effective immediately after the hero, where users are deciding whether to continue.

— Audit Finding #3

~80MB of hero video assets caused significant mobile load delays, preventing content from reaching users at the most critical entry point.

Key Insight

Performance degradation occurred before value or trust could be communicated, causing users to drop off prior to reaching meaningful content.

— Audit Finding #4

Personas

Three Distinct Visitor Mindsets, One Unified Experience

Three core user personas emerged from the research, each with distinct motivations and decision-making criteria. The redesign needed to serve all three without forcing any single path.

The Exotic Owner

High-value client focused on protection, quality, and trust.

Needs: Premium credibility + proven results

The Track-Day Driver

Performance-driven user seeking technical expertise.

Needs: Clear capabilities + engineering expertise

The New Enthusiast

Exploring upgrades with lower confidence.

Needs: Education, reassurance & entry points

Three Strategic Focus Areas

Clearer Value, Earlier Trust, and a More Direct Path to Conversion

The redesign was guided by three core focus areas: clarifying the value proposition, introducing trust earlier in the experience, and creating a more direct path for users to take action.

These priorities helped translate early discovery insights into a more structured, conversion-focused direction across the site.

Key Observations

Through early discovery, several patterns emerged that informed the direction of the redesign:

  • Opportunities to clarify messaging earlier, helping users quickly understand the offerings
  • Trust signals were more effective when introduced earlier in the experience
  • Some interaction-driven layouts limited visibility of core services
  • Conversion opportunities could be surfaced sooner for high-intent users
  • Mobile performance played a key role in first impressions and engagement

UI Design

Every Module Designed to Keep Users Moving Toward Conversion

The homepage was rebuilt as a conversion system — not a brochure. Each module serves a specific role in the user journey.

Dual Conversion System

Each module tested a different conversion hypothesis — authority-first vs. intent-first.

Module A: Authority-first approach emphasizing credentials and trust signals at top of page

Module B: Intent-first approach leading with service offerings and immediate CTAs

Social Media Integration

We added a live social media feed to the homepage, pulling S Collective's most recent posts directly into the browsing experience. This shows visitors the brand is active and current (a trust signal), and creates a content loop.

Blog & Content System

We built an entire blog into the S Collective website and surfaced it through a homepage blog module. This increases user retention, establishes authority, and creates an SEO foundation. When blog posts embed YouTube videos, it creates a content loop across Google and YouTube.

Intent-Based Contact Page

We replaced the generic contact form with an intent-based system routing users by need: Mechanical Services, Race Prep & Coaching, or Vehicle Sourcing & Sales.

Racing Page

Designed for Two Audiences at Once

Two audiences, one page. First-timers needed reassurance; enthusiasts needed credentials. Both find their path without scrolling past content that isn't for them.

The dual-audience structure held — both user types reached their primary action without additional navigation. The Racing page became one of the highest-converting pages in the redesigned site.

The Racing page structure was validated against both personas — reducing hesitation for first-timers by leading with approachability, and increasing confidence for enthusiasts by surfacing professional credibility before conversion. A single page serving two distinct intents, without compromise.

Video Production

A 20-Second Story That Does the Selling Before a Single Word Is Read

The original homepage hero relied on a static carousel. We replaced it with a produced hero video that tells S Collective's story in 20 seconds — establishing desire, credibility, and capability passively. The video was built with a UX narrative structure: aspiration → capability → invitation.

Technology & Optimization

Under-the-Hood Upgrades That Make the Site Faster, Lighter, and Scalable

A redesign that looks better but performs worse isn't a redesign — it's a liability.

MUX — Enterprise Video Infrastructure

We integrated MUX to handle all video content. Instead of loading from the web server, MUX streams through an optimized CDN with adaptive bitrate delivery — faster loads, smoother playback, and no performance degradation as more video content gets added.

Expected Impact

Designed to Convert — Built to Measure

Every design decision was made to reduce friction, build trust faster, and shorten the path from interest to action. Our target is a 20–40% increase in conversions within 90 days of launch, validated through GA4 and Microsoft Clarity.

What We're Measuring

We've configured two tracking platforms to monitor the site for the first 90 days:

GA4 (Google Analytics 4):

  • CTA click-through rate across service pages and homepage hero
  • Form completion rate measured against the pre-launch baseline
  • Session duration and exit rates on mobile vs desktop
  • Scroll depth tracking to confirm users reach key conversion sections
  • Full user path analysis from landing to form submission

Microsoft Clarity:

  • Session recordings showing real user behavior, clicks, and scroll patterns
  • Heatmaps revealing where users engage and drop off
  • Rage click and dead click detection
  • Mobile vs desktop behavior comparison

At the 30, 60, and 90-day marks, we'll review the data against the 20–40% conversion target and optimize. This isn't a launch-and-leave project.

What's Next

The Launch Is the Starting Line — Not the Finish

With the redesign complete and tracking in place, the next phase is optimization. Based on the first 90 days of data, we'll identify which conversion paths are performing, which pages have the highest drop-off, and where the next round of improvements should focus.
