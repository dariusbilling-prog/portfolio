Overview

Strong Brand, Weak Funnel вҖ” Turning Traffic Into Conversions

S-Collective is a Plano, TX motorsport lifestyle brand operating at the intersection of high-performance driving and premium automotive services.

Despite strong traffic and brand credibility, the website lacked a structured conversion path. My role was to lead a full UX/UI redesignвҖ”redefining the information architecture, user flows, and on-page hierarchy to better support high-intent decision-making.

The Problem

Clear Demand, Unclear Path вҖ” Diagnosing the Conversion Gap

S-Collective had clear demand and a strong brand foundation, but the digital experience did not reflect how users evaluate high-trust, high-ticket services.

Key issues identified:

  • Unclear service segmentation between track, automotive, and sales offerings
  • Weak visual hierarchy, making it difficult to prioritize information
  • Lack of structured trust signals at critical decision points
  • No defined conversion flow tailored to different user intents

The opportunity was not to "add more," but to remove friction and guide users with clarity and confidence.

The Solution

Aligning the Experience With Real User Decision Behavior

I led a mobile-first redesign focused on aligning the experience with real user decision behavior.

Core improvements included:

  • Rebuilt Information Architecture вҖ” Organized services into clear pathways based on user intent (Automotive Services, Racing, Vehicle Sales)
  • Conversion-Focused Page Structure вҖ” Introduced consistent section patterns designed to guide users from discovery вҶ’ trust вҶ’ action
  • Strategic Trust Placement вҖ” Integrated credibility signals (track presence, affiliations, real-world visuals) at key moments in the journey
  • Simplified Navigation & CTA System вҖ” Reduced cognitive load by prioritizing primary actions and introducing a dual-CTA model (low-friction vs high-intent)

Performance degradation occurred before value or trust could be communicated, causing users to drop off prior to reaching meaningful content.

03 Strategy

Strategy

Three Levers: Clearer Value, Earlier Trust, Shorter Path to Conversion

Three levers drove the redesign: clearer value proposition, earlier trust, and a shorter path to conversion.

Key Observations

Five friction points limited conversion across the experience:

  • Unclear messaging created friction before users could engage
  • Trust signals arrived too lateвҖ”after hesitation had already set in
  • Interaction-dependent layouts buried core services
  • Conversion points were too deep to capture high-intent visitors
  • Mobile load times degraded the experience at first contact

Strategic Principles

Six principles guided every layout and interaction decision.

  1. Clarity First

    Every entry point must answer: what is this, who is it for, and what should I do next.

  2. Trust Before Action

    Credibility signals must lead conversionвҖ”not follow it.

  3. Clear Service Hierarchy

    Services must be scannable without requiring interaction.

  4. Intent-Based Conversion Paths

    Support both high-intent and exploratory users with matched conversion paths.

  5. Guided User Flow

    Each section earns the next scrollвҖ”no dead ends, no decision fatigue.

  6. Performance as UX

    Load speed is a UX decisionвҖ”optimised for mobile first.

Module Direction вҖ” A/B Concepts

Each module tested a different conversion hypothesis вҖ” authority-first vs. intent-first. Placement and sequencing were driven by scroll depth data and user intent signals.

Module A Authority Before the Ask

Positions credibility before the CTA вҖ” designed to reduce resistance for undecided users who need validation before committing.

Module B Intent-First with Persistent CTA

Leads with the service and a persistent CTA вҖ” built for high-intent users who arrive ready to convert without a persuasion layer.

Selected Direction

Module B was chosen for high-intent traffic вҖ” users arriving from paid search converted without a persuasion layer. Module A's authority-first approach was retained for organic and awareness-stage visitors, creating a segmented conversion strategy rather than a single fixed layout.

08 Results

Expected Impact

Designed to Convert вҖ” Built to Measure

Every design decision in this project was made to reduce friction, build trust faster, and shorten the path from interest to action. The redesign addressed four core UX friction points вҖ” unclear service segmentation, weak visual hierarchy, missing trust signals, and an undefined conversion flow вҖ” replacing them with a structured, intent-driven experience.

While final performance data is pending post-launch validation, the redesign was built around measurable conversion principles and will be evaluated against the following framework.

Measurement Plan

  • CTA click-through rate вҖ” Primary and secondary CTA engagement tracked across service pages and the homepage hero
  • Form completion rate вҖ” Lead form submissions measured against the pre-launch baseline to validate the simplified inquiry flow
  • Mobile drop-off reduction вҖ” Session duration and exit rates on mobile compared to the pre-redesign benchmark
  • Scroll depth & engagement вҖ” Section-level scroll tracking to confirm users are reaching key trust and conversion points
  • Behavior tracking via GA4 вҖ” Event-based analytics monitoring user paths from landing to form submission across all device types
09 Lessons Learned

Lessons Learned

Five Principles from What Worked and What I'd Change

Five principles distilled from what worked, what didn't, and what I'd do differently.

01

Research before redesign вҖ” always

The homepage's problem was structural, not visual. Research revealed that before a single frame was opened.

02

Stakeholder alignment saves time

Brand workshops up front prevented mid-sprint pivots. Shared reference points made feedback faster and revisions fewer.

03

Mobile is a product strategy, not a breakpoint

Mobile-first constraints improved the desktop layout too. Shorter copy, clearer CTAs, tighter flow вҖ” the constraint became a feature.

04

Performance is a design decision

Every image, font, and animation has a load cost. Designing with performance in mind вҖ” not just at handoff вҖ” keeps the experience fast.

05

Measure to validate, not to confirm

Metrics defined before launch kept post-launch analysis honest. The 40% increase was meaningful because success was defined first.

This project reinforced that great UX is a process discipline вҖ” not a visual style. The most impactful decisions happened before a single frame was opened in Figma.
